MAKING THINGS HAPPEN.

When I became your First Minister last year, I knew that we had some work to do. Voters across Wales were making it clear to me that you felt we had lost touch – that your Government needed to refocus.

That is why the first thing I did was to embark on a listening tour. I came to your high streets, community centres, schools, hospitals and more to hear from you about the things that you thought the Government should be doing. And in doing so, I came up with a list of four priorities – detailed down below – that you wanted the Government to focus on.

And then I got to work.

In the months since, my Government has been making things happen, delivering on those priorities, and below you can find everything we have done so far.

I've listened, I've heard. And now, I'm making things happen.

Eluned Morgan.
First Minister of Wales and Leader of Welsh Labour.

Iechyd Da – A Healthier Wales

Creating a healthier Wales is Welsh Labour's number one priority. In the Welsh Labour Government's 2025 budget we had increased funding for the Welsh NHS by £600m, and are cutting NHS waiting times, including for mental health; improving access to social care and services for women’s health. Our achievements since Eluned Morgan became First Minister include:

  • £50m to cut long hospital waiting times
  • £21m for diagnostic equipment, to speed up testing and treatment
  • 5.5% pay award to NHS staff
  • Women’s Health Plan launched
  • All-Wales Fracture Liaison Service - a new way of treating broken bones, rolled out to every health board.
  • North Wales Medical School opened and successfully recruited its first direct intake of students.
  • Bowel cancer screening - thousands more people will automatically receive at-home tests as screening age lowered to 50 in Wales.
  • 50-day challenge to improve hospital discharge and community care - Health boards / LAs working together to help people return home from hospital.
  • New app to improve maternity care rolled out across Wales

Jobs and Green Growth

Creating jobs and growing our economy to tackle the problems of the future is a key priority for Welsh Labour. We are creating green jobs that tackle the climate crisis and restore nature, while making families better off; and are accelerating planning decisions to grow the Welsh economy. Our achievements on this since Eluned Morgan became First Minister include:

  • Wales at forefront of UK drive to ‘turbocharge’ AI - one of biggest data centres in Europe already in Newport, with plans for more.
  • Launched Wales and Japan 2025, to build economic and cultural links with Japan.
  • Action to accelerate infrastructure planning decisions. New proposals will allow infrastructure planning decisions of national significance to be taken more quickly.
  • 5 decisions on major new renewable energy projects approved since September - over 280 MW of renewable energy for Wales – equivalent to power 180,000 Welsh homes.
  • International Investment Summit, London – Wales at the top table, fighting for investment into Wales.
  • Broadband connectivity - £12m investment announced.
  • Shotton Mill, Deeside - secured investment of over £1bn for redevelopment, safeguarding 147 jobs and creating 220 more.
  • Launched the Independent Water Commission, the largest review of the sector since privatisation, alongside UKG.
  • £280,000 Forestry and Timber Skills fund to address skills gaps by subsidising places on accredited forestry and timber supply chain courses.
  • Farming - made £14m available to farmers for further SFS Preparatory Phase schemes.
  • £100,000 to help restore the equivalent of 266 rugby pitches of Welsh seagrass

Opportunity for Every Family

Another key priority for the Welsh Labour Government is ensuring that Wales is a great place for young people to grow up, and for families to get on. We are boosting standards in schools and colleges and providing more homes for social rent, ensuring every family has the chance to succeed. Our achievements on this since Eluned Morgan became First Minister include:

  • £120m extra to support local authorities (Dec 2024)
  • £20m extra for HE and FE sectors (Dec 2024)
  • Free School Meals - rolled-out to all pupils in maintained primary schools.
  • 5.5% pay award to teachers.
  • E-sgol - rolled out across Wales - video technology to connect classrooms so pupils can study subjects that may not have otherwise been available to them.
  • Literacy and Numeracy - additional £1.1 million to boost existing and successful programmes and provide enhanced support to teachers and learners.

Connecting Communities

The fourth priority for the Welsh Labour Government is ensuring that communities in Wales are well connected to one another. We are transforming our railways and delivering a better bus network; fixing our roads and empowering local communities to make choices on the speed limits of local roads. Our achievements on this since Eluned Morgan became First Minister include:

  • 20mph - Flintshire County Council has become the first local authority to consult on roads where 20mph may change safely to 30mph, with proposals on two roads:
    • A5026 in Lloc
    • Part of the A548 Bagillt/Mostyn Road.
  • Launched task force to maintain long-term stability for Holyhead Port
  • This year, we will have completed the 17.7km dualling of the A465 Heads of the Valleys Road (Hirwaun to Dowlais), to improve accessibility, reduce journey times, provide extra resilience and reliability, and enhance road safety.
  • Invested £10m to transform town and city centres across Wales
